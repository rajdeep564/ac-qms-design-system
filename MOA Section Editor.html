2Sample preparationRich text

Weigh accurately about 0.10 g of the substance, previously dried, and transfer to a dry conical flask.

Dissolve in 50 mL of anhydrous glacial acetic acid, warming gently if necessary to effect solution, then cool to room temperature.

3Standard preparationRich text

Prepare the working standard solution using Glycine RS dried to constant weight, following the same dissolution steps as the sample preparation.

4Blank preparationRich text

Carry out a blank titration using 50 mL of anhydrous glacial acetic acid without the substance under examination, and note the volume of titrant consumed.

5Reagent preparationRich text

Standardise 0.1 M perchloric acid against potassium hydrogen phthalate prior to use. Prepare crystal violet TS as indicator.

9ProcedureRich text + table

Equilibrate the chromatographic system with mobile phase until a stable baseline is obtained. Confirm system suitability before proceeding.

  1. Inject the blank and record the chromatogram.
  2. Inject six replicates of the standard preparation and verify all system suitability criteria are met.
  3. Inject the sample preparation in duplicate, bracketing with standard injections.
  4. Measure the peak areas for the principal peak in the sample and standard chromatograms.
